How It Works

A limited question router, not an open chatbot.

Type a question in plain English. The page groups similar phrasing into a small set of known intents and returns one of a few prepared answers. If it cannot answer properly, it sends people to the contact form.

  • No live LLM call, so there is no token leakage problem.
  • Answer count is capped per visit to keep it bounded.
  • It is useful for fit, scope, pilot, and positioning questions.
  • If the question is unusual, specific, or commercially important, it points people to the form.
  • If someone cannot be bothered with any of that, they can skip straight to the form.

Quick Answers

The questions that matter before a first conversation.

Fit

Who is quickler for?

Teams that visit sites, gather job evidence, and still spend too much time turning that into formal reports, records, approvals, or paperwork later. That includes inspection, survey, maintenance, compliance, field service, and operations teams.

Product

What does quickler actually do?

It helps teams turn notes, photos, voice notes, and other captured job information into finished reports or structured workflow outputs faster. On the AI-agent side, it also builds systems that load the right context before helping with repeated operational work.

Positioning

Is this just another AI writing tool?

No. quickler is focused on repeated workflows where structure, evidence, memory, and review matter. The point is grounded workflow help, not open-ended content generation.

Audience

Does quickler only work for engineers?

No. Engineers can be a good fit, but the better description is broader: quickler is for repeated workflows where the job happens first and the formal reporting or paperwork still gets rebuilt later.

Stage

What stage is the product at?

quickler is in early pilot stage. The current focus is proving a few narrow workflow shapes properly rather than pretending to be a giant finished platform.

Pilot

How do pilots start?

The starting point is one repeated workflow, a short explanation of how it runs now, and a few real examples if they can be shared. That is enough to judge whether a pilot makes sense.
