Typical problems

Repeated analysis, document work, and brittle handovers

This is for businesses where people keep reading the same inputs, re-explaining the same context, or rebuilding the same outputs because the current tools do not hold enough working memory.

Typical output

Agents shaped around the actual job

That might mean report-drafting agents, document-review agents, internal copilots, intake triage, research assistants, approval-flow helpers, or domain-specific assistants grounded in the team’s real source material.

Approach

Context first

The aim is to give the agent the right files, rules, boundaries, and workflow role before it does anything useful. The customer should care that the output is grounded and dependable, not that the demo sounds clever.

Examples of the sort of work this covers

  • Agents that turn notes, messages, and files into structured draft outputs
  • Internal copilots that answer with business-specific context instead of generic guesses
  • Document or approval agents that read, route, and prepare the next action
  • Research or triage agents that pull together the right source material before responding
  • Private domain agents shaped around one operator, one team, or one workflow with clear boundaries

What a customer should expect

  • An agent shaped around the real process, not a generic chatbot with a nice wrapper
  • Clear scope around one useful business problem first
  • Explicit context, rules, and review points where accuracy matters
  • Something practical enough that staff can trust and use

Best fit

This suits SMEs, charities, publishers, operational teams, and technical businesses that have repeated internal friction and can point to one workflow where a context-aware agent would clearly outperform ad hoc prompting.

How to enquire well

A strong enquiry explains the current workflow, what context the agent would need, where the current process breaks down, and what “better” would look like in plain terms.
