Workflow Fit

What the product should do in practice.

What it is for

This is for businesses where the facts are gathered during the job, but the proper report, record, admin pack, or next action is still assembled afterwards from notes, photos, voice notes, files, measurements, or memory.

What it should do

  • Let staff record the job properly while they are on site or in the flow of work, through a simple capture workflow
  • Shape that material into the right report format, document template, or workflow state
  • Keep final review with the person responsible for the report
  • Save time without making the process feel vague, risky, or less reliable

What makes a good pilot

The best starting point is one repeated report, document flow, or operational handoff, enough volume for the pain to be worth fixing, and real examples of the current process. That gives quickler enough structure to improve the workflow without asking your team to gamble on something vague.

Typical Examples

The workflow shapes this page is really about.

Field reporting

Inspection reports with repeated sections and evidence

Useful where staff capture notes, defects, photos, readings, or observations on site and then spend time rebuilding that into a formal report later.

Context-aware workflow tools

Internal tools that need rules, memory, and grounded outputs

Useful where a generic chatbot is not enough because the system has to load the right files, follow the right boundaries, and produce useful work inside a repeated process.

Approvals and admin

Repeated compliance, QA, approval, and document handling

Useful where evidence, traceability, and human accountability matter, but the current process still burns too much desk time and needs a clearer, faster way of working.

Good fit

Repeated workflows with a stable shape

Inspection reports, survey summaries, maintenance visit reports, site investigation records, QA packs, approval flows, and repeated document handling with a clear repeated shape.

Not ideal

No stable workflow pattern

If every case is materially different, or if review, evidence, and repeated structure are barely part of the job, this is probably not the right first fit.
