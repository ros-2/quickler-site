About Quickler

Quickler is moving from delivered workflow systems into a reporting product.

The company started by fixing messy internal workflows for real organisations. That work exposed a sharper repeated pain: technical teams still capture information on site, then rewrite it later into formal reports. Quickler Reports is the product direction that came out of that.

Quickler story

From workflow consulting to Quickler Reports

Quickler began by building practical workflow systems for organisations with repeated admin and document friction. The next step is productising the strongest repeated problem: technical reporting that gets written twice.

Founder

Philip Ross

Philip Ross founded Quickler in Dundee, Scotland. He studied Electrical and Mechanical Engineering at the University of Strathclyde and later worked at Dijuno, an AI analytics startup, building experience in LLM testing, prompt engineering, and Python automation. That engineering and workflow background is what pushed Quickler toward a serious reporting product rather than a generic AI wrapper.

Skills and builds

Built from real workflow pain

The product direction is informed by real delivered systems: reporting automation, structured documents, operational tracking, and workflow handoffs that had to work in practice rather than just demo well.
