About Quickler

Quickler exists to make repeated reporting much easier to finish.

Technical and field-based teams still capture notes, photos, and evidence during the job, then lose time turning that material into finished reports later at a desk. Quickler is focused on closing that gap.

Quickler story

Built around one repeated problem

The business is centred on a simple pattern: teams capture information in the field, then rewrite it later into formal reports. Quickler Reports is being built to remove that second pass.

Founder

Philip Ross

Philip Ross founded Quickler in Dundee, Scotland. He studied Electrical and Mechanical Engineering at the University of Strathclyde and later worked at Dijuno, an AI analytics startup, building experience in LLM testing, prompt engineering, and Python automation. That engineering and workflow background is what pushed Quickler toward a serious reporting product rather than a generic AI wrapper.

Skills and builds

Built from real reporting pain

The product direction is informed by work that had to handle reporting, structured documents, operational tracking, and handoffs in practice rather than just demo well.

Why now

The site is for pilot sign-up

Quickler is not trying to sell broad consulting work from this site. The goal is to find a small number of early teams with repeated reporting pain and start pilots with them.
