About Quickler

Quickler exists to make operational work feel clear again.

Many organisations run important processes through spreadsheets, inboxes, manual checks, and workarounds that technically function but quietly waste hours every week. Quickler turns those repeat-heavy workflows into simple internal systems that reduce friction, cut errors, and give teams time back.

Quickler story

From repeat-heavy admin to calmer operating systems

Quickler replaces spreadsheet chains, inbox bottlenecks, and fragile manual checks with focused internal tools built around how the work actually happens.

Founder

Philip Ross

Philip Ross founded Quickler in Dundee, Scotland. He studied Electrical and Mechanical Engineering at the University of Strathclyde and later worked at Dijuno, an AI analytics startup, building experience in LLM testing, prompt engineering, and Python automation.

Skills and builds

Systems built from practical engineering

His work combines workflow software with a broader habit of building useful systems from the ground up: automation tools, internal platforms, product prototypes, data-heavy applications, local-first software, and personal training technology such as his AI cycling coach.
